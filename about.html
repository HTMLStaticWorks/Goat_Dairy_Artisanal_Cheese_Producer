Our Heritage & Story

Learn how a humble family farm transformed into a state-of-the-art sustainable artisanal cheese producer.

Generational Integrity

The Legacy of Clover Meadow

In 1994, the Vance family settled in the lush green foothills of Gourmet Valley with twelve Alpine goats and a dream to restore organic micro-dairy practices. While industrial farming pushed for hyper-automation, we chose the slow, hand-pressed methods of traditional cheese artisans.

Today, Capra spans over 200 acres of biodynamic pasture. We still know our goats by name, hand-ladle our curds daily, and age our wheels in custom limestone caves that mimic the natural microclimates of French cheese cellars.

Ethical Stewardship

Our Production & Welfare Philosophy

We believe that milk quality is a direct reflection of an animal’s quality of life. Our goats pasture freely on native grasses, chicory, and clover. They sleep in deep-straw bedding and enjoy clean, fresh mountain well water.

Timeline of Growth

The Founding Herd

Julian Vance imports 12 elite Alpine goats to Gourmet Valley, beginning small-batch farmstead cheese testing.

2005

First National Gold Medal

Our Herb Chèvre wins first prize at the American Cheese Society competition, launching our brand into gourmet grocery circles.

Clean Energy Transition

We install 120kW solar systems and transition the entire milking barn to localized wind and green grid systems.

2026

Regenerative Dairy Leader

Celebrating 30+ years of high-welfare dairy farming, servicing over 120 wholesale distribution partners nationwide.
